Top Travel Destinations


  1. Rajasthan - The State of Forts

    2. Jaipur
    Udaipur
    Jaisalmer

    • Best Time to Visit - October to March

    • Sun Protection:
      Don't forget sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat or scarf to protect yourself from the sun, especially during the warmer months.
    • Comfortable Footwear:
      Choose comfortable shoes suitable for walking, exploring, and possibly desert safaris.
    • Consider Walking and Rickshaws:
      Explore local areas on foot or by rickshaw for a more immersive experience.


  2. Kerala - God's Own Country

    3. Munnar
    Allepy
    Wayanad

    • Best Time to Visit - September to March

    • Sun Protection:
      Don't forget sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat or scarf to protect yourself from the sun, especially during the warmer months.
    • Packing Essentials
      Pack light, breathable cotton clothing as Kerala has a tropical climate.
      If visiting hill stations like Munnar or Thekkady, bring some warm clothes as well.
      Don't forget beachwear, flip-flops, sunscreen, and mosquito repellent, especially if visiting the backwaters or wildlife areas.
    • Local Customs & Etiquette:
      Be mindful of dress codes when visiting temples and places of worship.
      Dress modestly, and remove your shoes before entering religious sites.
      Smoking and drinking alcohol are not permitted in public places.


  3. Meghalaya - The abode of Clouds

    4. Shillong
    Cherrapunji
    The Living Root Bridges

    • Best Time to Visit - November to February

    • Itinerary:
      Plan your itinerary carefully, considering the distances between attractions, which can involve long drives on hilly terrains.
    • Accomodation
      Book accommodations in advance, especially in popular destinations like Shillong and Cherrapunji, as they can be limited
    • Practical Tips:
      Cash: ATMs are scarce in rural areas, so carry sufficient cash for local transactions.
      Health: Take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, especially during the monsoon, using insect repellent and considering anti-malarial medication if traveling to remote areas.
      Safety: Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft.
